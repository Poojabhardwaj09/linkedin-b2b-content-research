
Title: How to STAND OUT on LinkedIn | Profile + Content tips with Justin Welsh


Link: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rAHaXzfy5nA

Transcript:

---

I kind of think of it in a way as like building a house for lack of a better analogy. You kind of have to have the foundation in place, and to me the foundation is your profile. It’s what people see when they come to interact with you and decide whether or not you’re worthy of their time.

So it’s really about getting the profile nice and clean. Make it very clear who you are, who you help, what you help them do, and how you help them do it. If people can’t figure that out, it becomes harder for them to follow you.

Clean profile:

* Good banner image
* Good headshot
* Strong tagline
* Clear about section
* Featured section with website/newsletter

Next, think about what you want to be known for.

You can’t talk about sales one day, your favorite animal the next, and random personal things after that. You need consistency.

What should your audience expect from you?

I always say:
“Learn how to say one thing a thousand ways, instead of saying a thousand things one way.”

If you do that, you become known for something specific. Then you attract people interested in that topic, and that helps grow your career.

---

Now about LinkedIn headline:

There’s no perfect way, but generally:

Instead of:
“CEO at company” → not useful

Use:

* Who you help
* What you help them do
* How you help them

Or:
“What will I get if I follow you?”

Example approach:
“I write about X”
“Follow me for Y”

Another approach:
Show a mission.

Example:
“Building a portfolio of one-person businesses to $5M revenue”

That makes people want to follow your journey.

---

Important point:

Your headline appears:

* Under your name in posts
* Under your name in comments

So it acts like a **constant promotion**

---

Next: Creator funnel idea

People go through stages:

1. Awareness
   They discover you (via LinkedIn, Twitter, etc.)

2. Trust
   They start believing you know your stuff
   → Newsletter helps here (deep thinking)

3. Relationship

* Community
* Events
* Calls

This builds stronger connection.

---

Key idea:
Move audience OFF platforms → into your own system (email list)

---

Problem:

Platforms don’t like links → reduce reach

Solutions:

* First comment links
* Featured section
* Profile link
* Writing link without hyperlink (like “justinwelsh dot me”)

---

Strategy example:

Pre-sell newsletter:
“Here’s what you’ll learn…”

Post-sell:
“25,000 people just learned…”

Use numbers → increases trust and interest
