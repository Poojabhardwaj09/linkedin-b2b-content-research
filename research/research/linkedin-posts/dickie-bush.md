So, what is this little-known but highly lucrative skill?

Lean Writing.

It's where you take a piece of proven content and expand it into a longer-form asset:

→ Atomic Essay
→ Newsletter
→ Books
→ Digital Product

But how do you expand an idea into all of the above assets?

Use the 10 Magical Ways.

Every idea can be expanded by adding:

1. Tips
2. Lessons
3. Benefits
4. Reasons
5. Mistakes
6. Examples
7. Questions
8. Data Points
9. Personal Stories
10. Step-By-Step Frameworks

But how do you get started?

Follow these 4 simple steps:

1. Start with what's already working on social media.

There’s a treasure trove of validated ideas EVERYWHERE, just waiting to be expanded:

• An IG story that got thousands of likes
• Or a Twitter thread that went semi-viral
• Or a LinkedIn post that got 500 comments

So look at your own analytics.

There will be data points telling you *exactly* what your readers want more of.

2. Take these ideas and expand them into Atomic Essays.

Now you're testing if people want to hear the expanded version.

Here’s how to write an Atomic Essay:

• Craft an intriguing headline.
• Outline the key points of the core message of your essay.
• Expand on your main points.

If you can write an Atomic Essay, you can write anything.

3. Create a weekly newsletter based on successful posts.

Again, you're just expanding your proven ideas.

But how do you know what to write in the newsletter? 

Easy!

• Have a look in your analytics
• Take a proven idea and expand it into a weekly newsletter
• Expand the idea with the 10 Magical Ways (as I described above)

And to make it 10x quicker, use AI to help you.

4. Scale into paid digital products and courses.

You already have all the foundational content:

• The social posts proved the initial interest
• The Atomic Essays provided the detailed framework
• The newsletters validated the idea further

And now, you're just expanding into a premium offering—a product!

90% of digital products fail before they launch.

And it has nothing to do with your:

• Platform
• Marketing
• Or pricing

It comes down to one simple mistake:

Starting from vagueness instead of specificity.

I've seen 1,000s of creators do this.

They brainstorm product ideas inside giant mega-categories like:

• Healthcare
• Technology
• Productivity
• Financial Services

The problem?

These categories are MASSIVE.

Inside "healthcare" alone, you could be talking to:

• Doctors
• Physical therapists
• Healthcare executives
• HealthTech startup founders
• Or even health insurance providers

Each group has completely different problems, goals, and pain points.

When you try to solve problems for "everyone in healthcare," you end up solving problems for no one.

The fix?

Niche down aggressively.

Mega-Category → Niche → Niche-Within-Niche

For example:

• Healthcare
• Physical therapy
• Physical therapy for remote workers

Physical therapy for remote workers.

And suddenly, the problems your digital product can solve become painfully obvious:

• "Every time I lift my toddler, my lower back tweaks."

• "I sit at a desk 10+ hours a day and my hips are so tight I can't sleep."

• "My neck and shoulders are constantly stiff from back-to-back Zoom calls."

See the difference?

Instead of fixing "healthcare," you're solving three specific, painful problems for one very specific person.

The more specific you get, the easier everything becomes.
