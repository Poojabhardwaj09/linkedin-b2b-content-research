I made $5,000 with my first digital product.

But I lost out on $1M (with one painful mistake):

The mistake?

I didn't use *email* to sell it.

Crazy, right?

For context, over the last 6 years, I’ve generated $15,000,000 in digital product sales.

And the VAST majority of those sales have come via email.

But I only sold my first digital product over social media.

Which meant I only had a narrow window to sell the product.

But with email, you can keep putting your offer in front of your customer again and again.

So here’s how you should think about attracting attention to your product and selling it to your customers:

1. You use social media to attract attention and get people onto your email list

2. You use your email list to sell your product

Now, the second part isn’t about spamming your email list with sales emails 24/7.

No, it’s all about using a weekly newsletter to drive sales to your digital product.

What should you send each week?

Here's the simplest answer I can give you:

• "More" of what you already write about
• "More" of what's working
• Just "more"

That’s literally all it has to be.

Now, if that isn't clear enough, let's make this EVEN MORE actionable.

Make sure your weekly newsletter does these 4 things:

1. Has 800-1,500 words

Less than this and it's going to feel a little "thin."

More than this and it's going to feel too big of a lift to write.

2. One topic per newsletter

Each newsletter should be its own asset.

And in order for each newsletter to be its own asset, that means each newsletter needs to be focused on only tackling 1 topic at a time.

The more you cram into each one, the less helpful.

3. Problem or outcome-oriented

Next, you want each newsletter to speak to 1 of 2 things:

• 1 of the 10 Biggest Problems you’ve identified for this target reader/potential customer

• 1 of the 10 Most Desirable Outcomes you’ve identified for this target reader/potential customer

In short, your free-value newsletter should help them solve these 10 biggest problems and/or unlock these 10 desirable outcomes…

FOR FREE.

4. Organically mention your digital product

This is key.

In every newsletter, you want to organically mention your low-ticket digital product:

• It could be a more direct call to action
• It could be a more passive mention

Or both.

This is your entire Weekly Newsletter Strategy.

How to build a 7-figure digital product in 7 dead-simple steps:

(even if you have no writing skills)

My first digital product only made $5,000.

But my second one generated over $3,000,000.

And since then, I've launched products doing 6 and 7-figures in revenue.

This is the system I use to create digital education products:

Step 1: Name the modules

All digital products are just a series of modules, strung together, to help a customer achieve a specific goal.

So, start backward:

• What's the end goal?
• What are the steps to get there?

Make each step a module.

Step 2: Prep The Page

Here's an outline you can use to "prep" an entire module:

• "In this module, we'll cover..."
• "To start, avoid these mistakes..."
• "Instead, take these action steps..."
• "You probably have these questions/objections..."
• "Here's your assignment!"

Step 3: Write each section

Once the page has been "prepped," all you have to do is fill it in!

Remember your toolkit:

• 1/3/1 Writing Rhythm
• Single-sentence openers
• Use subheads to separate sections
• Alternate paragraphs & bulleted lists

Make it easy to skim!

Step 4: Add Q&A After Each Section

Pro tip here...

After every section, your reader is going to be faced with 2 obstacles:

• Questions: "But what do I do if...?"
• Objections: "I don't agree with that because..."

Pause and address these before continuing on.

Step 5: Mock Example Looms

Don’t just “tell” the reader how to do something.

Show them!

• Pretend you’re where they are on the journey
• Perform the action step you’re telling them to take
• Record yourself doing exactly what you’re telling them to do

This helps them “imagine” themselves doing it.

Step 6: Loom Review (Of Your Example Loom!)

Here’s where it gets meta…

• Record a Loom
• Watching your Mock Loom
• Pointing out & explaining “why” you’re doing all the little things you’re doing.

This takes the reader 3 layers deep—and helps them internalize what’s happening.

Step 7: Finish Each Module With An Actionable, Measurable Assignment

The ONLY measure for success for your digital product is whether customers DO something with it.

• Give them an Assignment
• Make it objective & binary
• Hold them accountable

Action > Information

Bonus Step 8: Get Beta Customers ASAP!

Do not spend 6+ months trying to assemble the perfect product.

Get V1 in people’s hands as quickly as possible—and then iterate:

• Where are people confused?
• Where are people stuck?

Then, revamp to V2.
